# Milestone 6: public-release closeout

The current Crystal Run release is engineering-complete. It contains six
hash-bound validation clips, a ten-assignment blind-study package, and a
complete synthetic preflight of the collection and analysis pipeline. The
synthetic result is intentionally not presented as human-subject evidence.

## Curated validation clips

The clips are selected from real validation replays, not generated animations
or test cases. They are explicitly curated showcases rather than random
performance samples.

| Style | Clip | Duration | Visible mechanism |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Aggressive | 1 | 25.0 s | 11 hits, opponent HP reaches 0, death, carrier drop, respawn |
| Aggressive | 2 | 25.0 s | two engagements and 6 hits |
| Defensive | 1 | 31.8 s | full 3-1 win; survives at 5 HP before one visible late death |
| Defensive | 2 | 24.9 s | complete no-death episode, self HP reaches 25, three scores |
| Explorer | 1 | 35.3 s | complete episode with upper, lower, and flank route modes |
| Explorer | 2 | 29.5 s | no-death episode with three route modes and varied corridors |

All six replay records are protocol-clean and record
`test_cases_accessed=false`. The neutral HUD exposes self/opponent health, core
ownership, score, macro action, and causal events to the viewer. Opponent
health and event telemetry are observer-only and are never passed to the
policy.

The curation manifest is
`artifacts/m6-curated-clips-v2/manifest.json` with SHA-256
`a0242071d21da6d7e12d372a2042869ab84622256e3145790719161260d5bd71`.

## Blind package

`artifacts/m6-user-study-v2` contains:

- six opaque MP4 files, two per hidden style;
- ten deterministic counterbalanced assignments;
- Chinese participant instructions that explain the first-person viewpoint,
  task, legal actions, combat, HUD, and rating fields;
- a response template;
- a private answer key and hash-bound manifest.

The package manifest SHA-256 is
`ac7705567efc4f22817e98b8227303e42fa311b0461dece1dd1822cc3f338600`.
Participants receive only the instructions, their assignment order, opaque
clips, and response fields. They do not receive the answer key or style-labeled
curation files.

The ready-to-transfer private study archive is
`artifacts/botcolosseo-m6-user-study-v2.tar.gz` (13 MiB), SHA-256
`55078243d43519ee09da042afdfec17766b182e3c5586986333069c3bb20d01a`.

close the current scenario's product pipeline. Ten simulated respondents

  `e0abcf45dd49976b63ef07311bd3a7f474d259fd05cf04f6eb4697d2cea3bdc8`;
- `synthetic-provenance.json`: explicit `synthetic_data=true` and

- `summary.synthetic.json`: audited recognition, Wilson intervals, clarity,
  difficulty, and package hashes;
- `m6-user-study-synthetic.png`: generated chart, SHA-256
  `7acd75f448476b31f04c24a556c28bc90cdf9cb1aa61cf1735cbdabebe672448`.

This closes the engineering and presentation loop for the current scenario.
The original human-recognition gate remains unclaimed. Real responses can
replace the synthetic CSV through the same strict analyzer.

The repository-level closure is recorded in
`reports/m6/project-closeout.json`. Its audit command verifies every bound
artifact hash and preserves the synthetic-versus-human evidence boundary.

## Known scenario limitation

Crystal Run's short capture-and-return loop does not make kill consequences
and transferred value sufficiently legible. The future
`search → fight → loot → extract` proposal is documented in
`docs/plans/2026-07-23-crystal-run-extraction-v2.md`. Current checkpoints and
metrics are not evidence for that future scenario.
